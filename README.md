# Hi there, I'm An Tran ^^

I'm a student developer from Vietnam. I grew up in Đà Lạt, moved to Sài Gòn for university, and spent my first year not really knowing who to ask about anything — where to find a room, what to expect from a landlord, how any of it worked. A lot of what I build now comes straight from those small, lonely moments.

So most of my projects are just me trying to fix something I actually went through. Living alone got too expensive and there was no good place to look for a roommate — so I started **roomieVerse**. I kept watching a café put together its staff schedule by hand every week, someone always ending up unhappy — so I'm making **shiftbot**. And **Our Warmth** came from a night I still think about, when I wanted to help someone and didn't. Some of these are about code. Most of them are about a feeling I couldn't shake.

**How I build:** I lean on AI a lot — but I treat it like a very fast typist, not someone I hand the whole job to. I decide what we're making and why, I redo the same screen ten times until it *feels* right, and I deploy everything myself. I don't like putting my name on things I can't explain.

I'm also a bit of a perfectionist, and not always in a good way — roomieVerse has technically worked for months, but I still haven't opened it to the public because a few tiny flows aren't the way I want them yet. I'd rather be late than ship something confusing.

**A bit of backstory:** I've been chasing the *feeling* of good software since I was a kid — reinstalling Windows in 4th grade just to get that glossy Aero Glass look, wiring up my own home network in 7th grade because I loved how everything clicked together. Nobody made me do any of it. I just liked how it felt, and I still notice the tiny UX details most people walk right past.

I'm early in all this and honest about it. I'm looking for a junior role with a real team and people I can learn from.
